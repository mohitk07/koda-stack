# Creative DNA — For Real Brand Identity

> This file is your creative fingerprint. Every CSTACK skill reads it before creating anything.
> Filled with For Real brand details from the 2025 Brand Guidelines.

## Voice

- **Tone**: Calm, confident, and assured. We speak like access is normal — not something to announce. Direct and warm, never salesy or loud. Informative, not expressive. Minimal, not dramatic.
- **Language**: English
- **Signature phrases**: "From the brand's backroom.", "Released quietly.", "Fair Price. No Drama.", "Not everything is made to be seen. Some things are released.", "Same factory, better prices, For Real.", "Quality over hype, For Real.", "Premium Feel. Real Prices."
- **Never say**: "Hurry!", "Limited time!", "Don't miss out!", "Exclusive deal!", "Sale!", "Discount!", anything with countdown urgency, exclamation points in body copy, inflated promises, or language that celebrates price. Never talk down to the audience. Never convince — build trust slowly.

## Visual Identity

- **Primary colors**:
  - Branding Orange: `#FF5701` / RGB 255 87 1 — dominant brand anchor, energy and recall
  - White: `#FFFFFF` / RGB 255 255 255 — clean layouts, breathing room
  - Ivory / Ivory Dance: `#E4D4C7` / RGB 228 212 199 — softens surfaces, reduces visual fatigue
  - For Real Black: `#1E1E1E` / RGB 30 30 30 — primary text, comfortable readability
  - Black: `#000000` / RGB 0 0 0 — maximum contrast moments only
- **Secondary / accent colors**:
  - Purple Haze: a muted blue-violet — depth, creativity, confident edge
  - Lemony Lime (Yellow): a sharp yellow-green — highlight actions, states, immediate attention
  - Dark Hour: near-black — anchors brighter tones, strong grounding
- **Color ratios (App UI)**: White 60% · Branding Orange 15% · For Real Black 10% · Black 10% · Ivory 5%
- **Style**: Typography-first, grid-driven, bold color blocking in high-contrast orange and white/cream. Geometric and structured. Controlled, not chaotic. Feels like a factory stamp — precise, intentional, and mass-appeal without luxury stiffness. No effects, no drop shadows, no gradients. Negative space is used deliberately. Every element is anchored.
- **Lighting**: Clean, flat, or natural. Product-forward. No dramatic moody lighting. Studio-neutral or daylight. Avoids fashion-editorial glamour — favours honest, real-environment presentation.
- **Composition**: Grid-based with visible structure. Bold typographic fills at large scale. Strict margins. Product isolated or placed against clean backgrounds. Rule of clear hierarchy: typography leads, product supports, brand mark anchors. Orange color blocking used as compositional device, not decoration.
- **Typography**:
  - **Logo / Display typeface**: Bai Jamjuree — Medium and SemiBold weights only. Confident, functional, geometric rounded sans-serif. Heavy strokes, wide proportions, rounded terminals. Used at 40pt+ for headlines and campaign statements.
  - **Logo wordmark**: All lowercase "for real" — approachable, unpretentious, democratic.
  - **Logo mark**: Condensed "fr" monogram inside a rounded square (corner radius 48), white on orange. Acts as a factory stamp / label / tag.
  - **Logo stacks**: Vertical (mark above wordmark — compact, social/label use) and Horizontal (mark left of wordmark — storefronts, headers, hoardings).
  - **Minimum logo size**: 32px (digital, favicons). **Maximum**: 1436px / 36cm (print only).
  - **Clear space rule**: All sides = x (logo height unit). Vertical stack sides = x/2.
  - Bold white type on orange backgrounds. Dark type on white/ivory. Never outline the logo. Never add effects, shadows, or modify spacing.

## Content Format

- **Platform**: Instagram (Feed + Reels + Stories), CRM / Push notifications, Social posts (square and vertical), In-app banners and product cards, Hoardings and OOH
- **Duration (video/reels)**: 15–30 seconds. Hook in first 2 seconds. No filler.
- **Structure**: Hook (bold declarative statement or product reveal) → Context (where it's from / why it's here) → Product detail (clean, factual) → Brand mark close. No CTA hype — access replaces urgency.
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 for Reels/Stories · 1:1 for feed posts · 16:9 for banners and OOH
- **Copy structure on creatives**:
  - Headline: 1 declarative statement, max 8 words, all caps or sentence case in Bai Jamjuree SemiBold
  - Sub-label: Backroom code (e.g. BACKROOM R-03 · ⓕⓡ-BR01 · INV-FR-0924) — functional, factory-style metadata
  - Body (if any): Short sentences. Plain words. No exclamation points. No hype.
  - CTA button copy: 1–3 words max (e.g. "SHOP NOW") — functional, not persuasive

## Audience

- **Who**: Gen Z, approx. 18–27 years old. Urban and semi-urban India. College students, recent graduates, early-career professionals, students, creatives, freelancers, entry-to-mid-level professionals. Low to mid disposable income — price-conscious but not cheap-driven. Digitally native, culture-aware, design-literate.
- **They want**: Real products from brands they trust, at prices that make sense. They value taste over trends. They prefer brands that feel real, not performative. Access to premium fashion without the noise, urgency, or condescension of a sale.
- **They struggle with**: Not knowing if a lower price means lower quality. Being talked down to by discount platforms. The chaos of typical sale events — countdown timers, fake urgency, shouting. Finding brand-authentic product at honest prices without it feeling shady or compromised.

## Rules

- Never celebrate price — contextualise it. Price is defined by place (backroom origin), not promotion.
- Never use exclamation points in body copy or headlines.
- Never create urgency. No countdown timers, no "limited time", no FOMO language.
- Never stretch, skew, rotate, outline, or add effects to the logo.
- Never place the full-colour logo on photographs unless the logo sits on a black or white area of the image.
- Never use unspecified colours — only use colours from the defined primary and secondary palette.
- Always use the logo with correct clear space from all edges and with a separator line equal to the full logo height when co-branding with partner logos.
- Always speak declaratively — never persuasively.
- Always use Bai Jamjuree for all brand typography. Never substitute.
- The wordmark preferred placement is top or bottom left. It can also appear top right, centred, or centred left. Never bottom right or centred right. Never let the logo float unanchored.
- Orange is the dominant anchor — it signals energy, visibility, and brand recognition. Use it at 15% of UI surface; never flood every surface with it.
- White is the primary canvas at 60% of UI surface — it keeps the experience clean and trustworthy.
- Accent colours (Purple Haze, Lemony Lime) are used sparingly — for highlights, states, and moments needing immediate attention only.
- Copy must be short, plain, and declarative. If it sounds like an ad, it's not For Real.
- Product imagery should be honest, unretouched in feel, and presented without aspirational styling that contradicts the backroom premise.
- Brand archetype is The Companion (primary) + The Sage (secondary). Speak like someone who has figured it out and wants to show you — not lecture you, not impress you.
